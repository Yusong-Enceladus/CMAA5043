🔬 Why does my robot dog fall over?
Great question! Your robot falls over because of something called center of gravity. When the heavy parts are too high or too far to one side, gravity pulls them down! That's why we made the base wide and flat — to keep the weight balanced.
🌟 Fun Fact: Real dogs use their tails to help balance, just like we added a tail to your robot!
💻 How do real robots see?
Real robots use sensors as their eyes! Some use cameras (like the one on a phone), and some use special sensors that measure distance using invisible light. That's why we added the sensor brick to the front — it's your robot dog's eyes!
🌟 Fun Fact: Some robots can see in the dark using infrared light that humans can't see!
⚙️ Why did we use different-shaped pieces?
Each shape has a special job! Flat plates spread out the weight. Tall bricks add height and strength. Angled pieces let the legs bend. Engineers choose the right shapes just like this when building real things!
🌟 Fun Fact: Engineers who build real robots go to school for years to learn which shapes work best!
🎨 Can I make my robot look different?
Absolutely! Art and design are a huge part of building robots. You can choose different colors, add decorations, and even change the shape of the head to give your robot personality! Every great robot starts with a creative designer.
🌟 Fun Fact: Companies like Pixar use real robots as inspiration for their movie characters!
🔢 How many pieces did we use?
Let's count! We used 23 pieces in total: 8 for the body, 8 for the legs (2 each!), 4 for the head, and 3 for the tail. That's addition! And we used symmetry — each side looks the same. Symmetry is when both sides match like a mirror!
🌟 Fun Fact: The word "symmetry" comes from Greek and means "same measure"!
